JANTAR MANTAR
| Qutub Minar | India Gate | Safdarjung Tomb | Jama Masjid | Humayun's Tomb | Red Fort | Jantar Mantar
Structure type: Observatory
Built by: Maharaja Jai Singh II of Jaipur
Architechtural style: Astronomical
Construction period: 1724
Location: Connaught Place

Jantar Mantar consists of 13 architectural astronomy instruments with 723 feet height. The primary purpose of the observatory was to compile astronomical tables, and to predict the times and movements of the sun, moon and planets. The site is one of four built by Maharaja Jai Singh II of Jaipur, others being in Jaipur, Ujjain and Varanasi. Only structure in the observatory which is not invented by Jai Singh II is the Misra Yantra.

Distinct Instruments
The Samrat Yantra, or Supreme Instrument, is a giant triangle that is basically an equal hour sundial.
Jaya Prakash Yantra consists of hollowed out hemispheres with markings on their concave surfaces which help observer align the position of a star.
Ram Yantra is used to measure the altitude of stars based on the latitude and the longitude on the earth.
Misra Yantra is a composition of 5 instruments designed as a tool to determine the shortest and longest days of the year. It could also be used to indicate the exact moment of noon.
